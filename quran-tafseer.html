Surah Al-Baqarah, Verse 255 - Ayatul Kursi

The Verse of the Throne
اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ ۚ لَّهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۗ مَن ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِندَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَئُودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ

Allah - there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of [all] existence. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Who is it that can intercede with Him except by His permission? He knows what is [presently] before them and what will be after them, and they encompass not a thing of His knowledge except for what He wills. His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth, and their preservation tires Him not. And He is the Most High, the Most Great.

Tafseer Insights from Shia Sources

According to Tafseer Al-Mizan by Allama Tabatabai, this verse establishes the absolute sovereignty and knowledge of Allah. The term "Al-Qayyum" (the Sustainer) indicates that Allah is the foundation upon which all existence depends, not just in creation but in continuous sustenance.

Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (A.S) has explained that "Kursi" (Throne) in this context refers to Allah's knowledge and authority that encompasses the entire creation. The verse emphasizes that no intercession occurs without Allah's permission, establishing the principle that all intermediaries function by divine will.

This verse is considered one of the greatest in the Quran for its comprehensive description of Allah's attributes and is often recited for protection and blessings according to traditions from Ahlulbayt.

- Based on Tafseer Al-Mizan, Tafseer Al-Burhan, and traditions from Ahlulbayt (A.S)

Renowned Shia Tafseer Scholars

Learn from the works of great Shia exegetes throughout history in our Tafseer program

Allama Tabatabai

1903-1981

Author of Al-Mizan fi Tafsir al-Quran, one of the most comprehensive modern Tafseer works that uses the Quran to interpret the Quran, with extensive references to traditions from Ahlulbayt.

Shaykh Tabarsi

1073-1153 CE

Compiler of Majma' al-Bayan, one of the most celebrated classical Shia Tafseer works known for its comprehensive approach combining linguistic analysis, traditions, and rational arguments.

Allama Majlisi

1627-1699 CE

Compiler of Bihar al-Anwar, the Ocean of Lights, an encyclopedic collection of traditions from Ahlulbayt including extensive commentary on Quranic verses and their Quranic interpretations.
